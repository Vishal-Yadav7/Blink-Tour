Hidimba Devi Temple

Sun, May 11th 2024
Hidimba Devi Temple, also known as Hadimba Temple, is a unique and ancient temple located in Manali. Here's what you should know about this historical site:
  • Direction

   History of Hidimba Devi Temple

    Sun, May 11th 2024
 The temple is dedicated to Hidimba Devi, a character from the Indian epic Mahabharata and    the wife of Bhima, one of the Pandava brothers. According to mythology, Hidimba performed severe penance to attain divinity in this region.
  • Direction

Architecture of Hidimba Devi Temple

Sun, May 11th 2024
The temple is renowned for its unique architecture, which is a blend of wooden and stone structures. It is built in a pagoda style with intricately carved wooden doors and walls. The temple's roof is made of brass and features a three-tiered pagoda-shaped structure.
  • Direction

   Religious Significance

    Sun, May 11th 2024
 Hidimba Devi Temple holds great religious significance for the locals and is visited by devotees seeking blessings. The temple is especially crowded during the annual festival of Dhungri Mela, which is held to celebrate the goddess's birthday.
  • Direction

Surroundings and Location

Sun, May 11th 2024
The temple is located amidst dense deodar forests, adding to its serene and tranquil ambiance. The lush greenery and scenic surroundings make it a popular spot for tourists and nature lovers.
  • Direction

   Nearby Attractions

    Sun, May 11th 2024
 Nearby attractions include the Manu Temple, which is dedicated to the sage Manu, and the Manali Sanctuary, a wildlife sanctuary known for its diverse flora and fauna.
  • Direction

Tips for Visitors

Sun, May 11th 2024
1. Footwear: Visitors are required to remove their footwear before entering the temple.
2. Photography: Photography is allowed outside the temple premises but restricted inside the temple.
3. Respect: Visitors are expected to maintain decorum and respect the religious sentiments of the devotees.
Hidimba Devi Temple is not just a religious site but also a symbol of architectural and cultural heritage, making it a must-visit destination for tourists visiting Manali.
